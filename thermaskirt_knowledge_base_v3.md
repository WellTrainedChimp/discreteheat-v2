## 2. PRODUCT FUNDAMENTALS

### 2.1 What ThermaSkirt Is

An aluminium radiant heating panel disguised as a skirting board. In effect, a radiator that has been squashed, stretched and reshaped into a skirting profile. It replaces both the skirting board and the radiator in one component.

20mm thick, available in up to 6-metre lengths, virtually indistinguishable from an ordinary skirting board. Delivers radiant heat at low level across the room perimeter.

### 2.2 How It Works — Technical Principles

**Radiant vs Convection:** Traditional radiators primarily rely on convection currents. When flow temperatures are reduced (as heat pumps require), radiator output drops by ~70% from dT50 to dT20. Example: 1000W radiator at dT50 = only 300W at dT20. ThermaSkirt has no fins to convect heat, so output retention is far better: 1000W at dT50 = 480W at dT20.

**Thermal Envelope:** Heating systems offset heat loss, not heat occupants. By emitting heat at the external perimeter, ThermaSkirt creates a thermal envelope trapping heat within the room. Thermal comfort is unaffected by furniture layout — radiant heat brings all objects to equilibrium.

**BSRIA Evidence:** Comparative thermal topography: 800W via K2 radiator showed 17.1–22.0°C variation across the room. 800W via radiant skirting showed 19.7–20.5°C — practically indistinguishable from underfloor heating. Less than 1°C variance vs up to 5°C with radiators.

**Air Quality Benefit:** Radiant heat from low down produces dramatically less air movement than convection radiators, meaning significantly less dust, allergens and airborne particulates circulating. Customers with allergies and asthma report noticeably cleaner air and better sleep quality.

## 3. CORE BENEFITS

### 3.1 Space & Layout Freedom

Eliminates radiators — reclaims 600–900mm wall space per radiator. At 20mm thick, projects less than timber skirting. Furniture goes anywhere without blocking heat (thermal envelope principle). In London, floor space can cost £25,000/m² — removing one radiator can reclaim £2,000 of usable space.

### 3.2 Even Heat Distribution

BSRIA tested: less than 1°C variance vs up to 5°C with radiators. Comparable to underfloor heating comfort. No hot zones near emitter, no cold corners.

### 3.3 Low Temperature / Heat Pump Performance

Designed for 35–45°C flow temperatures. Retains ~48% output at dT20 vs ~30% for radiators. Ideal heat pump partner — supports maximum COP. Avoids oversized radiators that are heavy, ugly and impractical. Can supplement existing radiators: if a room needs 1kW but existing radiator provides 600W at low temp, add 400W of ThermaSkirt rather than upsizing the radiator.

### 3.4 Boiler Efficiency & Gas Savings

Works with any boiler (gas, LPG, oil, biomass). Because ThermaSkirt has more surface area, it delivers same heat at lower flow temps (typically 50–55°C vs 70–80°C for radiators). This keeps condensing boilers in condensing mode, recovering waste heat from flue gases. Result: up to 20% lower gas bills without changing the boiler. Works with standard boiler controls — Hive, Nest, Tado, TRVs, programmers.

### 3.5 Quick Response Time

Low water volume (0.48 L/m BM2, 0.72 L/m BM3) = heat-up and cool-down in minutes, not hours. Typically reaches target temperature in 20–30 minutes. Contrasts with underfloor heating which takes hours. Supports Part O/TM59 overheating compliance through rapid off-response.

### 3.6 Silent Operation

No expansion clicks, ticking, banging or gurgling. Genuinely silent. Critical for bedrooms (undisturbed sleep) and home offices (video calls, concentration).

### 3.7 Air Quality

Radiant heat from low level = dramatically less air movement than convection radiators. Significantly less dust, allergens, and particulates circulated through breathing zone. Customers with allergies/asthma report noticeably better air quality and sleep.

### 3.8 Retrofit Simplicity

Over 70% (historically) of installations are retrofit. Installs above floor, connects to existing pipework. Bracket system copes with uneven walls/floors. Can be bent on site for severely bowed walls (listed buildings). Typical room installation: 1–2 days. 70% of domestic customers self-install (DIY) using YouTube guides and PDF instructions.

### 3.9 Safety

- Low surface temperature without additional covers (designed below 43°C safe limit)
- Eliminates 86–87% of radiator injuries (impact injuries per RoSPA data), mostly affecting under-5s and over-65s
- Larger radiators (as required for heat pumps) increase impact and trip risk — ThermaSkirt eliminates this
- Anti-ligature and anti-tamper options for healthcare/mental health settings

### 3.10 Hygiene

Sealed design eliminates dust traps. Minimal air movement reduces airborne pathogen spread. Cleaned during routine floor mopping. Developed with NHS infection control specialists. Addresses 2008 Journal of Hospital Infection findings. Optional anti-microbial coating for healthcare.

### 3.11 Damp, Mould & Awaab's Law

Radiators encourage clothes drying behaviour. On heat pumps, this reduces system efficiency, increases humidity, leads to condensation and mould. Removing radiators eliminates this behaviour, supporting compliance with Awaab's Law in social housing.

### 8.3 Installation

Connects to existing pipework same as radiators. Brackets mounted (1/m), ThermaSkirt clicked on, lengths joined via push-fit, TRV fitted, cover plates fitted. System pressure tested and commissioned.

**What arrives:** Long skirting lengths + small components box containing: installation instructions, cutting list (piece-by-piece breakdown with wall positions), feed/return kits, internal/external corner kits, brackets, gasket, covers, fittings.

**Tools needed:** Chop saw with metal-cutting blade, deburring/reaming tool (supplied in kit), pick tool (supplied), plastic pipe cutters, utility knife, tape measure (8m), pencil, pry bar, mallet, screwdrivers/screw guns. No specialist tools required beyond what's in the kit.

**Install sequence:** (1) Fix reflective foil to wall. (2) Mount brackets — first bracket ~200mm from feed point, then every 600–800mm. Fix method depends on wall: screw into timber, plasterboard fixings, stud fixings, or metal frame screws. (3) First-fix pipework using downloadable pipe template — supports floor or wall entry. 15mm plastic pipe recommended for flexibility; copper works but less forgiving. (4) Assemble feed/return kit (TRV top, lockshield bottom). TecTite push-fit connections to pipework. (5) Measure and cut panels per cutting allowance sheet. (6) Ream/deburr cut ends, grease O-rings with silicone, engage connectors. (7) Mount onto brackets — locate on lower ramp, push back to seat. (8) Commission: bleed via top vent, purge airlocks by opening bottom drain with top vent open, isolate other rads to clear remaining air. (9) Balance using lockshield (manual TRV) or at manifold. (10) Fit top rubber gasket when warm — lubricate with wet wipe, mitre at corners, bond with two-part glue + activator. (11) Clip cover plates from top. (12) Fit TRV valve fascia — includes locking child-safety mechanism and frost protection position.

**BM2 Cutting Allowances:** TRV feed/return: wall −120mm. Internal corner: wall −35mm/−20mm (asymmetric for expansion). External corner: wall +0–3mm. Return manifold: wall −60mm. Non-TRV/threshold: wall −100mm. In-line joints (>4.5/6m): wall −15mm. Odd angles: offer flexible coupling centrally and mark.

**BM3 Cutting Allowances:** TRV feed/return: wall −170mm. Internal corner: wall −35mm/−20mm. External corner: wall +0–3mm. Return manifold: wall −170mm. Non-TRV/threshold: wall −100mm. In-line joints: wall −15mm.

**Critical install note:** The cutting allowance + clip orientation reference sheet is the most important document. Clockwise install shown; for anti-clockwise, mirror dimensions. QR codes on sheets link to relevant video sections. Clips are orientated with the 'wings to the front', on all conncetions with the exception of TRV/Lockshield valves, or at internal 90 corner connection.

### 10.1 Why Heat Pump Conversions Stall

Energy Systems Catapult (DESNZ, March 2024) found: 68% of heat pump dropouts cited disruption, 15% practical issues, only 10% technical, only 4% cost. Total: 83% of failures were disruption or practicality — not technology. This is routinely overlooked by engineers and installers operating in a heat-pump echo chamber.

### 10.2 The Radiator Sizing Problem

Heat pumps run at 45–55°C vs 70–80°C for boilers, so radiators need to be 2–3× bigger. A typical 600×600mm bedroom radiator becomes 2000×600mm or larger. Living rooms may need 2400×600mm radiators — heavy (95kg wet weight), requiring wall reinforcement, visually industrial. Many installers won't mount them. 4.1 million UK homes have microbore pipework (8–10mm) that would need replacing for oversized radiators but works fine with ThermaSkirt.

### 10.3 The "Radiators Aren't Hot" Problem

At 50°C, radiators feel warm not hot. Residents interpret this as "broken" even when room temperature is correct. Local authorities report this as a major source of complaints. ThermaSkirt eliminates this expectation mismatch — radiant perimeter heating feels warm without relying on a hot-to-touch surface.

### 10.4 ThermaSkirt's Role

Reduces disruption (75% in local authority trial). Avoids oversized radiators — distributes output across perimeter at modest size. Can supplement existing radiators via Add2Rad (add ThermaSkirt to make up shortfall rather than replacing). Improves comfort (fewer complaints). Eliminates safety risks of larger radiators. Removes damp/mould risk. Works with existing pipework including microbore. Presents heat pumps as a lifestyle/comfort upgrade, not a compromise. Compatible with all major heat pump brands.

### 10.5 Add2Rad for Heat Pump Retrofits

Specifically designed for the supplementary use case. Connects in series to existing radiators. Boosts room output at low flow temps without replacing anything.
